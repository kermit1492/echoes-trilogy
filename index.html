Book I — Shadows of Sintra

Shadows of Sintra

After his grandfather's death, archivist Markus Valente finds seven reels of magnetic tape hidden in a tin box and a letter with no signature: "The tapes are waiting for you. Sintra, Rua das Sombras, 13. Do not trust the silence."

The tapes hold the voice of Isabella Correia — a journalist who traveled to Sintra in 1987 to investigate the tunnels beneath Quinta da Regaleira. She never came back. Her recordings did. Markus came to Sintra to fill a gap in his family's history. What he finds instead will fill a gap in him.

"Do not listen to the silence, senhorita. Sometimes it answers." — Senhora Rosa, keeper of the old inn
Chapter I

Read the Opening

The train clattered along the worn rails between Lisbon and Sintra like the dying mechanism of a bygone era. The carriage smelled of metal dust and damp that had soaked into the seat upholstery. Markus Valente sat by the window, his fingers nervously gripping the handle of a battered leather suitcase. Inside lay seven reels of magnetic tape — the only key to an archive his grandfather had kept in secret from everyone. This journey was not a choice for Markus; it was an inevitability. As an archivist, he could not leave the greatest gap of his life unfilled.

Sintra received him in silence — thick, tangible, as if the very air was wary. The fog seemed to be born from the stones themselves, clinging to the spires of the Moorish castle and dissolving into the emerald haze of the forests surrounding Quinta da Regaleira. Tourists came here seeking romance; Markus felt that the real Sintra was hiding in the folds of this fog.

He pulled a letter from his pocket, its paper already beginning to crumble: "The tapes are waiting for you. Sintra, Rua das Sombras, 13. Do not trust the silence." No signature. No hint of a sender. The letter had arrived one week after António Valente was buried in Coimbra.

Stepping onto the platform, he found himself in emptiness. Only an elderly woman in a black shawl, selling chestnuts, watched him with a long, searching gaze.

"Rua das Sombras?" she repeated when he asked. Her voice was dry as husks. "The Street of Shadows... Some shadows are best left alone, senhor."

She pointed silently toward the hill, where houses clung to one another as if in fear of something unseen.

House number 13 looked like a skeleton eaten by time: three floors of peeling plaster, windows shuttered and boarded. Above the roof, a decrepit turret rose incongruously, its window a gaping hole of broken glass. Markus knocked.

The door opened a crack, revealing a hunched old man in a long, unseasonably warm overcoat. His face resembled parchment.

"Markus Valente?" The old man's voice was quiet, like the rustle of crumbling dust.

"Yes. And you are?"

"Doctor José Almeida. I've been waiting. Come in."

The Seekers

Those Who Listened

Markus Valente
The Archivist
An archivist from Lisbon who has spent his life cataloguing the past. When his grandfather dies, he leaves behind seven magnetic tapes and a letter pointing to Sintra. Markus follows — driven by the one gap in history he cannot leave unfilled.
Isabella Correia
The Voice on the Tapes
A journalist from Porto who traveled to Sintra in 1987 to investigate the tunnels beneath Quinta da Regaleira. She recorded seven tapes. Then she vanished. Her voice is the only thing that survived.
Rafael Sousa
The Anchor
Historian, skeptic, Markus's oldest friend. Born in Sintra, he knows its legends better than anyone. He has his own reasons for returning — reasons he has never spoken about.

There are others. In other cities. Looking for the same thing.

Setting

Sintra, Portugal

This is where it begins. Later, the geography changes — from fog to glass towers, from tunnels to the spaces between stars.

Place
Quinta da Regaleira
A labyrinthine estate of grottoes, initiation wells, and underground tunnels carved into the hills of Sintra. A place where architecture, alchemy, and mythology intertwine — and where the boundary between the real and the imagined thins to nothing.
Place
Rua das Sombras, 13
The Street of Shadows. A narrow lane where houses lean into one another as if afraid of something unseen. Number 13 stands at the end — three stories of peeling plaster, shuttered windows, and a crumbling tower with a broken pane of glass.
Object
Seven Magnetic Tapes
Found in a tin box on a dead man's attic. Only the first bears a label: "Sintra, 1987." They contain the voice of a woman who disappeared thirty years ago. What else they contain is the question Markus should not have asked.
Atmosphere
The Fog
In Sintra, fog is not weather. It is landscape. It rises from the stones, clings to the castle spires, and fills the forests with an emerald haze. Tourists find it romantic. Those who stay too long find it something else entirely.
The Question

How far would you go to find the answer to the question that has haunted you since childhood?

Some stories are about heroes who fight evil. This is not one of them. This is a story about the price of knowing — and whether the mind that finally reaches the truth can still recognize itself. It begins in a house in Sintra. It does not end on this planet.
